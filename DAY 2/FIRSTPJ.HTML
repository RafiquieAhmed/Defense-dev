How to Crack a Job with Zero Knowledge

please these steps are followed

Step 1: Understand Company Expectations

  • Service-based companies-(TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Capgemini, Cognizant): They mainly check your aptitude, basic coding (easy-level DSA), and computer fundamentals.
  • Mid-level companies-(Mindtree, Accolite, Harman, ZS, etc.): They test coding skills (DSA up to medium-level), OOPs, DBMS, OS, and problem-solving.
  • Big MNCs/Product companies-(Amazon, Microsoft, Adobe, etc.): They expect strong DSA (medium–hard), system design knowledge (basic for freshers), and deep understanding of CS fundamentals.

Step 2: Subjects You Must Cover

  • Data Structures & Algorithms ->Start with arrays, strings, searching, sorting, then move to linked list, stack, queue, recursion, trees, graphs, dynamic programming. Learn time and space complexity.
  • DBMS and SQL ->Learn normalization, joins, transactions, indexes. Practice writing SQL queries on platforms like HackerRank.
  • Operating Systems ->Understand processes, threads, scheduling algorithms, deadlocks, memory management.
  • Computer Networks ->Learn OSI vs TCP/IP, HTTP/HTTPS, DNS, TCP vs UDP.
  • OOPs concepts ->Master encapsulation, inheritance, polymorphism, abstraction, with examples in C++/Java/Python.
  • Aptitude (especially for service-based) -> Quantitative aptitude (time & work, probability, permutations), logical reasoning, and verbal ability.

Step 3: Resources You Can Use to given test

  1. Aptitude: R.S. Aggarwal book, PrepInsta, IndiaBix.
  2. DSA & Coding:GeeksforGeeks (topic-wise), LeetCode (for practice), Coding Ninjas/TakeUForward (YouTube playlists).
  3. Core CS: Neso Academy or Gate Smashers YouTube for DBMS, OS, CN.
  4. SQL: HackerRank SQL track.
  5. Interview Prep: Pramp or InterviewBit for mock interviews, Glassdoor for company-specific questions.

Step 4: Suggested Timeline (around 4 months)

  • Month 1: Focus on aptitude, easy-level DSA, SQL basics. You’ll be ready for service-based drives.
  • Month 2: Move to medium-level DSA, cover OS, DBMS, and OOPs. Prepares you for mid-level companies.
  • Month 3: Go deeper into advanced DSA and computer networks. Do mock tests and revise projects.
  • Month 4: Company-specific prep, polish resume, attempt mock interviews, and apply actively.

Step 5: Resume and Projects

Keep 2–3 strong projects (web app, ML model, DBMS-based system, etc.). Don’t just list mini-projects — write about the skills used and the impact of the project.

Step 6: Applying for Jobs

  1. For service-based: Attempt TCS NQT, Infosys InfyTQ, Wipro Elite, Capgemini, etc.
  2. For mid-level and MNCs: Apply through LinkedIn, company career pages, and referrals. Also participate in coding contests (Codeforces, CodeChef, HackerRank).
